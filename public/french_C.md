# FRENCH

## Course Objectives
1. To introduce learners to basic French language skills with emphasis on listening, speaking, reading, and writing.
2. To enable students to communicate effectively in simple everyday situations using basic French expressions.
3. To develop a foundational understanding of French grammar, vocabulary, and sentence structure.
4. To help learners acquire correct pronunciation and intonation through systematic practice of sounds and accents.
5. To familiarize students with French culture, social etiquette, and everyday practices to promote intercultural awareness.

---

## MODULE I: Introduction to French

### 1. Importance of French as an International Language
French is one of the most widely spoken languages in the world, spoken by millions of people across Europe, Africa, North America, and parts of Asia. It is an official language of international organizations such as the United Nations, UNESCO, NATO, the European Union, and the International Red Cross. Learning French helps in global communication, higher studies, diplomacy, tourism, and international trade.

### 2. French-Speaking Countries
French is spoken in more than 29 countries, which together form the Francophone world. Some major French-speaking countries include:
*   France (Europe)
*   Canada (especially Quebec)
*   Belgium
*   Switzerland
*   Luxembourg
*   African countries such as Senegal, Ivory Coast, Cameroon, and Congo

### 3. French Alphabet and Pronunciation
The French alphabet has 26 letters, the same as English, but the pronunciation is different:
*   **A** – ah
*   **B** – bay
*   **C** – say
*   **D** – day
*   **E** – uh
*   **F** – eff
*   **G** – zhahy
*   **H** – ash
*   **I** – ee
*   **J** – zhee
*   **K** – kah
*   **L** – ell
*   **M** – emm
*   **N** – enn
*   **O** – oh
*   **P** – pay
*   **Q** – koo
*   **R** – air
*   **S** – ess
*   **T** – tay
*   **U** – oo
*   **V** – vay
*   **W** – doo-bluh-vay
*   **X** – eeks
*   **Y** – ee-grek
*   **Z** – zed

### 4. Greetings and Leave-Taking Expressions
**Greetings:**
*   *Bonjour* – Good morning / Hello
*   *Bonsoir* – Good evening
*   *Salut* – Hi (informal)
*   *Bonjour Madame* – Good Morning Madam
*   *Bonjour Monsieur* – Good Morning Sir

**Leave-Taking:**
*   *Au revoir* – Goodbye
*   *À bientôt* – See you soon
*   *Bonne journée* – Have a good day

### 5. Self-Introduction
Here are basic self-introduction phrases in French:
*   *Bonjour Madame et Monsieur* – Good morning/afternoon Sir/Madam
*   *Je m'appelle...* – My name is...
*   *Je suis étudiant(e).* – I am a student. (e.g., *Je suis étudiant(e) en B.Tech.*)
*   *J’ai 18 ans.* – I am 18 years old
*   *Je viens de...* – I come from... (e.g., *Je viens de l’Inde* / *Je viens de Telangana/Andhra Pradesh*)
*   *Je suis en première/deuxième/troisième/quatrième année.* – I am in first/second/third/fourth year
*   *Je me spécialise en [informatique / mécanique / électronique].* – My specialization is [computer science / mechanical / electronics]
*   *J'aime apprendre de nouvelles technologies et améliorer mes compétences en communication.* – I like learning new technologies and improving my communication skills
*   *Mes passe-temps sont...* – My hobbies are...
*   *Je parle anglais, un peu de français et un allemand approximatif.* – I speak English and a little French and broken German
*   *Merci beaucoup.* – Thank you very much

## MODULE II: Basic Grammar

### 1. Numbers (1–10)
*   **1** - un
*   **2** - deux
*   **3** - trois
*   **4** - quatre
*   **5** - cinq
*   **6** - six
*   **7** - sept
*   **8** - huit
*   **9** - neuf
*   **10** - dix

**Days of the Week:**
*   *lundi* (Monday)

*   *mercredi* (Wednesday)
*   *jeudi* (Thursday)
*   *vendredi* (Friday)

**Months:**
*   *janvier* (January)

*   *mère* – mother
*   *frère* – brother
*   *sœur* – sister
*   *grand-père* – grandfather

### 4. Professions
*   *étudiant(e)* – student
*   *professeur* – teacher
*   *médecin* – doctor
*   *ingénieur* – engineer
*   *commerçant(e)* – shopkeeper

## MODULE III: Vocabulary and Expressions

### 1. Gender and Plural Forms of Nouns
French nouns are either masculine or feminine.
*   **Masculine articles:** un / le (e.g., *un garçon* - a boy)
*   **Feminine articles:** une / la (e.g., *une fille* - a girl)

### 2. Personal Pronouns
*   **Je** – I
*   **Tu** – You (singular)
*   **Il** – He | **Elle** – She
*   **Nous** – We
*   **Vous** – You (plural/formal)
*   **Ils** – They (masculine) | **Elles** – They (feminine)

---

## MODULE IV: Sentence Formation and Communication

### 1. Talking about Daily Activities
*   *Je me réveille* – I wake up
*   *Je vais à l’école* – I go to school
*   *Je mange* – I eat
*   *Je dors* – I sleep

### 2. Describing People, Places, and Objects
*   *grand / grande* – big
*   *petit / petite* – small
*   *beau / belle* – beautiful
*   *intelligent(e)* – intelligent

---

## MODULE V: Functional French and Culture

### 1. At the Market and Shopping
*   *Combien ça coûte ?* – How much does it cost?
*   *Je veux acheter…* – I want to buy…
*   *C’est cher.* – It is expensive.
*   *C’est bon marché.* – It is cheap.

### 2. At a Café or Restaurant
*   *Je voudrais un café.* – I would like a coffee.
*   *Le menu, s’il vous plaît.* – Menu, please.
*   *L’addition, s’il vous plaît.* – The bill, please.

### 3. Asking for Directions
*   *Où est… ?* – Where is…?
*   *À droite* – to the right
*   *À gauche* – to the left
*   *Tout droit* – straight ahead

### 4. Travel and Basic Communication
*   *Je suis touriste.* – I am a tourist.
*   *Aidez-moi, s’il vous plaît.* – Please help me.
*   *Je ne comprends pas.* – I don’t understand.
*   *Parlez-vous anglais ?* – Do you speak English?
