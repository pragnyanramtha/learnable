## Unit 3: Clocks and Calendars

### Short Answers

**Q: At what time between 5 to 6 O’clock will the hands of a clock point coincide with each other?**
**A:** Using the formula $\theta = |30 \times H - \frac{11}{2} m|$:
$0 = |30 \times 5 - \frac{11}{2} m|$
$150 = \frac{11}{2} m \Rightarrow m = \frac{300}{11} = 27 \frac{3}{11} \text{ minutes}$
The time will be 5 hours and $27 \frac{3}{11}$ minutes.

**Q: What will be the angle between the hour and minute hands of the clock at 7:30?**
**A:** $\theta = |30 \times H - \frac{11}{2} m|$
$\theta = |30 \times 7 - \frac{11}{2} \times 30| = |210 - 165| = 45^\circ$.

**Q: If it is 5:40 in the 12 hours clock then what will be the time in the mirror?**
**A:** Mirror Time $= 11:60 - \text{Actual Time}$
$11:60 - 5:40 = 6:20$. The mirror time is 6:20.

**Q: If the water image of the clock shows 3 hours 25 minutes, then what will be the actual time?**
**A:** Actual Time $= 6:30 - \text{Water Image Time}$
$6:30 - 3:25 = 3:05$. The actual time is 3:05.

**Q: If 12th July 2010 is a Monday, what was the day of the week on 12th July 2009?**
**A:** Using the day code formula: $\frac{12 + 0 + 6 + 9 + 2}{7} = \frac{29}{7} \Rightarrow \text{Remainder } 1$. Remainder 1 corresponds to Sunday.

**Q: The angle between the minute hand and the hour hand of a clock when the time is 4:20?**
**A:** $\theta = |30 \times H - \frac{11}{2} m|$
$\theta = |30 \times 4 - \frac{11}{2} \times 20| = |120 - 110| = 10^\circ$.

**Q: At 3:40, the hour hand and the minute hand of a clock form an angle?**
**A:** $\theta = |30 \times H - \frac{11}{2} m|$
$\theta = |30 \times 3 - \frac{11}{2} \times 40| = |90 - 220| = 110^\circ$.

**Q: Today is Monday. After 61 days, it will be?**
**A:** Convert days into weeks: $61 \div 7 \Rightarrow 61 = 8 \times 7 + 5$.
$8 \text{ weeks} = 56 \text{ days} \rightarrow \text{same day (Monday)}$.
Counting 5 days after Monday (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday) gives Saturday.

### Long Answers

**Q: A watch gains 4 seconds in 3 minutes and was set right at 8 AM. What time will it show at 10 PM on the same day?**
**A:** Time from 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM is 14 hours.
$14 \times 60 = 840 \text{ minutes}$.
The watch gains 4 seconds in 3 minutes, so the number of 3-minute intervals is $\frac{840}{3} = 280$.
Total gain $= 280 \times 4 = 1120 \text{ seconds}$.
Converting seconds to minutes: $1120 \div 60 = 18 \text{ minutes } 40 \text{ seconds}$.
Adding the gain to actual time: $10:00 \text{ PM} + 18 \text{ min } 40 \text{ sec} = 10:18:40 \text{ PM}$.

**Q: The clock was set on Thursday, at 4 AM. If the clock gains 20 minutes per hour, then what will be the time that the clock shows on Friday, 8:30 PM?**
**A:** Actual time elapsed from Thursday 4:00 AM to Friday 8:30 PM is $24 + 16.5 = 40.5 \text{ hours}$.
Total gain $= 40.5 \times 20 = 810 \text{ minutes}$.
$810 \div 60 = 13 \text{ hours } 30 \text{ minutes}$.
Adding the gain to the actual time (Friday 8:30 PM + 13 hours 30 mins) results in 10:00 AM on Saturday.

**Q: If March 1st, 2024 is a Saturday, what day of the week will be September 1st, 2024?**
**A:** Count days from March 1 to September 1: $31 + 30 + 31 + 30 + 31 + 31 = 184 \text{ days}$.
Convert into weeks: $184 = 26 \times 7 + 2$.
$26 \text{ weeks} \rightarrow \text{same day (Saturday)}$. Move 2 days after Saturday (Sunday, Monday). The day will be Monday.

**Q: What was the day on 14 April 2000?**
**A:** Using the formula calculation: $\frac{14 + 0 + 6 + 00 + 0}{7} = \frac{20}{7} \Rightarrow \text{Remainder } 6$. Remainder 6 corresponds to Friday.

**Q: What was the day on 16 August 1947?**
**A:** Using the formula calculation: $\frac{16 + 3 + 0 + 47 + 11}{7} = \frac{77}{7} \Rightarrow \text{Remainder } 0$. Remainder 0 corresponds to Saturday.

**Q: A watch which gains 5 seconds in 3 minutes was set right at 7 a.m. In the afternoon of the same day, when the watch indicated quarter past 4 o'clock, the true time is?**
**A:** Time shown from 7:00 AM to 4:15 PM is 9 hours 15 minutes, which equals $555 \text{ minutes}$.
Number of 3-minute intervals $= \frac{555}{3} = 185$.
Total gain $= 185 \times 5 = 925 \text{ seconds}$.
Converting to minutes: $925 \text{ seconds} = 15 \text{ minutes } 25 \text{ seconds}$.
The true time is $4:15:00 - 15:25 = 3:59:35 \text{ PM}$.

**Q: What was the day of the week on 11th March, 2026?**
**A:** Using the formula calculation: $\frac{11 + 4 + 6 + 26 + 6}{7} = \frac{53}{7} \Rightarrow \text{Remainder } 4$. Remainder 4 corresponds to Wednesday.

**Q: On what dates of April, 2001 did Wednesday fall?**
**A:** 1 Jan 2000 was a Saturday. Since 2000 was a leap year, 1 Jan 2001 was a Monday.
Total days up to April 1, 2001 $= 31 + 28 + 31 = 90 \text{ days}$.
$90 = 12 \times 7 + 6 \text{ odd days}$.
Starting from Monday, adding 6 days gives Sunday. Since 1 April 2001 was a Sunday, Wednesdays fell on the 3rd, 10th, 17th, and 24th.

**Q: If for a certain quantity of books, the publisher has to pay Rs. 30,600 as printing cost, then what will be amount of royalty to be paid for these books?**

$20 : 15 = 30600 : r \Rightarrow r = \text{Rs. } (\frac{30600 \times 15}{20}) = \text{Rs. } 22,950$.

$120 : 25 = 180 : n \Rightarrow n = \text{Rs. } (\frac{25 \times 180}{120}) = \text{Rs. } 37.50$.

**Q: If 5500 copies are published and the transportation cost on them amounts to Rs. 82500, then what should be the selling price of the book so that the publisher can earn a profit of 25%?**

$10 : 125 = 82500 : x \Rightarrow x = \text{Rs. } (\frac{125 \times 82500}{10}) = \text{Rs. } 1031250$.
S.P. of one book $= \text{Rs. } (\frac{1031250}{5500}) = \text{Rs. } 187.50$.

**Q: Royalty on the book is less than the printing cost by:**
**A:** Difference $= (20\% \text{ of C.P.}) - (15\% \text{ of C.P.}) = 5\% \text{ of C.P.}$
Percentage difference $= (\frac{5\% \text{ of C.P.}}{20\% \text{ of C.P.}} \times 100)\% = 25\%$.

## Unit 5: Logical Thinking and Pattern Analysis

### Short Answers

**Q: In a row of students, John is 8th from the left end, and Mary is 12th from the right end. Calculate the total number of students in the row.**
**A:** Total students $= (\text{John's position from left}) + (\text{Mary's position from right}) - 1 = 8 + 12 - 1 = 19$.

**Q: Radha selected the 27th card from the left in a row of 50 cards. What will be the position of the same card from the right?**
**A:** Position from right $= 50 - 27 + 1 = 24$. The card is 24th from the right.

**Q: In a row of 54 persons, A is 35 from left side and B is 22 from right. Find the total number of persons between them.**
**A:** B's position from left $= 54 - 22 + 1 = 33$.
Difference in positions from left $= 35 - 33 = 2$.
Persons between them $= 2 - 1 = 1$ person.

**Q: One morning Udai and Vishal were talking to each other face to face at a crossing. If Vishal's shadow was exactly to the left of Udai, which direction was Udai facing?**
**A:** Udai was facing North.

**Q: Reena walked from A to B in the East 10 feet. Then she turned to the right and walked 3 feet. Again she turned to the right and walked 14 feet. How far is she from A?**
**A:** Using the Pythagorean theorem: $\text{Required distance} = \sqrt{3^2 + (14 - 10)^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = 5 \text{ ft}$.

**Q: If SYMBOL is written as NZTMPC is it a certain code? How is NUMBER written in that code?**
**A:** The pattern splits the word in half and applies a cross +1 increment mapping. Following the visual representation, "NUMBER" would be written as "NVOSFC".

**Q: If South-East becomes North, North-East becomes West and so on. What will West become?**
**A:** Following the rotational shifts on the compass diagram, West will become South-East.

**Q: Sachin walks 20 km towards North. He turns left and walks 40 km. He again turns left and walks 20 km. Finally he moves 20 km after turning to the left. How far is he from his starting position?**
**A:** Required distance $= 40 - 20 = 20 \text{ km}$.

**Q: From his house, Lokesh went 15 km to the North. Then he turned west and covered 10 km. Then he turned south and covered 5 km. Finally turning to the east, he covered 10 km. In which direction is he from his house?**
**A:** He is in the North from his house.

### Long Answers

**Q: In a School, there are 147 people, the ratio of girls: boys is 1:6. Soumya is a girl who stands 15th from the top of that row and 7 girls are in front of her. How many boys are behind her?**
**A:** Total ratio parts $= 1 + 6 = 7$.
Girls $= \frac{1}{7} \times 147 = 21$.
Boys $= \frac{6}{7} \times 147 = 126$.
Soumya is 15th overall with 7 girls in front, meaning she is the 8th girl.
Girls behind her $= 21 - 8 = 13$.
Total persons before Soumya $= 14$. Boys before her $= 14 - 7 = 7$.
Boys behind her $= 126 - 7 = 119$.

**Q: Sahil and Gaurav are standing in a row of persons. Sahil is 12th from left side and Gaurav is 18th from the right side of the row. If they interchanged their positions Sahil becomes 25th from left. What is the total number of persons standing in the row?**
**A:** Total persons $= (\text{Sahil's new position from left}) + (\text{Gaurav's original position from right}) - 1$.
$25 + 18 - 1 = 42 \text{ persons}$.

**Q: Rasik walked 20 m towards north. Then he turned right and walks 30 m. Then he turns right and walks 35 m. Then he turns left and walks 15 m. Finally he turns left and walks 15 m. In which direction and how many meters is he from the starting position?**
**A:** Analyzing his movements on a grid, his final required distance is $30 + 15 = 45 \text{ m}$. He is facing East from his starting position. Answer: 45 m East.

**Q: Two cars start from the opposite places of a main road, 150 km apart. First car runs for 25 km and takes a right turn and then runs 15 km. It then turns left and then runs for another 25 km and then takes the direction back to reach the main road. In the meantime, due to minor break down the other car has run only 35 km along the main road. What would be the distance between two cars at this point?**
**A:** Required distance $= 150 - (25 + 25 + 35) = 150 - 85 = 65 \text{ km}$.

**Q: A man walks 2 km towards North. Then he turns to East and walks 10 km. After this he turns to North and walks 3 km. Again, he turns towards East and walks 2 km. How far is he from the starting point?**
**A:** By creating a right triangle from his total net displacements (North: $2+3=5$, East: $10+2=12$), the required distance $= \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = 13 \text{ km}$.

**Q: In a certain code language, 'BLADE' is written as 'CKBCF' and 'CHALK' is written as 'DGBKL'. How will 'DIGITAL' be written in that language?**
**A:** The coding pattern is that each letter is replaced by the next letter in the alphabet ($+1$).
$D \rightarrow E$, $I \rightarrow J$, $G \rightarrow H$, $I \rightarrow J$, $T \rightarrow U$, $A \rightarrow B$, $L \rightarrow M$.
"DIGITAL" is coded as "EJHJUBM".

**Q: In a certain code language, 'MAKE' is written as '32' and 'QUAD' is written as '45'. How will 'SELF' be written as in that language?**
**A:** The logic adds 2 to the sum of the alphabetical positions of each word's letters.
MAKE $= 13 + 1 + 11 + 5 = 30$, and $30 + 2 = 32$.
QUAD $= 17 + 21 + 1 + 4 = 43$, and $43 + 2 = 45$.
Applying to SELF: $S(19) + E(5) + L(12) + F(6) = 42$.
$42 + 2 = 44$.

**Q: Y is in the East of X which is in the North of Z. If P is in the South of Z, then in which direction of Y, is P?**
**A:** Based on the geometric mapping (X is North of Z, Y is East of X, P is South of Z), P is located to the South-West of Y.
